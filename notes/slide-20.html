Beat: Walk through it briskly — one sentence per week. This makes the 3-week promise tangible.

Say: "Here's exactly how the three weeks run. Week one: we wire everything up — your email platform, RightMessage, intake survey on your thank-you page, click-tracking active. Week two: your new subscribers start profiling themselves. The welcome sequence fires automatically by intake answer. We also send a re-engagement email to your existing list so they get a chance to tell you who they are. Week three: we build your prospect map — every contact tagged by role, challenge, and buyer readiness. We finish with a sprint review call where I walk you through your first qualified cohort."

Watch out: Don't linger here. The point is to show the work is concrete and phased, not to explain every technical step. One pass, keep momentum.

If he asks about cutting it closer to launch: "Three weeks from kickoff still leaves you about a week of buffer before July 21st. I'd rather hand you a tested system with room to spare than promise faster and have you launching on day one of a brand-new setup."
