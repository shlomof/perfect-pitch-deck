Let this slide sit. Don't rush it.

Ask: "Peter — when your book drops and someone reads it, likes it, and subscribes to your newsletter — what happens next? Do you know who that person is? What they do? Whether they're a consulting prospect or just a curious reader?"

He doesn't. That's the point. Let the silence land. Then say: "That's the gap. You're building an audience of people who trust you — 7,000 already, more coming with the book. But there's no layer underneath telling you which ones are ready to hire you and which ones never will be."

He said it himself: the best outbound is still manual signal-hunting. You're mirroring his own words back at him.
