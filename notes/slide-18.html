What's Included — Slide 18

Beat: Six concrete deliverables, quickly — this is the "what am I actually getting" slide.

Say: "Concretely: works with however you already collect interest, a personalized nurture sequence, a dynamic site that matches each visitor, tagging so you know what someone's interested in before you call, a kickoff call to map your actual qualifying questions into the build, and ongoing tuning as more cities come online."

Scenario to use live: "If someone's looked at the Premium tier three times, that's tagged differently than someone who glanced at Land once. By the time you call them, you already know which conversation you're walking into."

Watch out: Don't pad with generic inclusions like "support" or "consultation" that every vendor claims. Card 1 is "Setup," not "Connect" or "Capture" — don't claim to plug into a specific existing form/system (event sign-up and website contact form may not even be the same system) or name a specific tool like Google Form — we haven't confirmed either with TID.

Expanded from 4 to 6 cards (2026-07-14): Shlomo asked whether 4 cards was too thin compared to Peter Winick's deck, which used 7. Checked that deck's committed version — it broke the build into granular real pieces (Re-Engage, RightMessage, Survey Build, Welcome Flow, Prospect Map, Kickoff Call, Sprint Calls) rather than 4 broad ones. Added two genuine, non-padded pieces here: "Kickoff Call" (a real first step — mapping their actual 3 qualifying questions into the build) and "Check-Ins" (the ongoing tuning that the monthly retainer is actually paying for, previously undocumented anywhere in the deck). Didn't invent a 7th — nothing else in scope is distinct enough not to read as padding.
